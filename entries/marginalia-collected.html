Marginalia, Collected

The scraps too short to be entries and too stubborn to throw away.

notebook fragments aphorism

Every notebook has a back drawer: the single sentences that arrived without a story attached, overheard or misremembered or invented at a red light and attributed, later, to the fog. Here are a few, filed under the headings they seemed to want.

On ambition

A ladder leaned against the wrong wall is still, technically, exercise.

On memory

The past is a country that keeps quietly redrawing its own borders, and never sends a new map.

Cf. The Cartographer of Small Hours. Apparently I only have the one metaphor and I am determined to wear it out.

On writing

The sentence you delete at midnight is the one you will spend all of the next morning trying to rebuild from memory, and it is never as good, and you deserved that.

On silence

Some rooms are quiet the way an empty house is quiet. Others are quiet the way a held breath is quiet. It is important, when entering a room, to establish which.

On kindness

Most of it is just paying attention out loud.

On endings

Not every thought earns a paragraph. Some are complete the moment they're written down, and the kindest thing you can do is let them stop.

— the drawer is never full; add your own to the bottom of this file.
