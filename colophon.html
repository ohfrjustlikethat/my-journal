Colophon·About this desk

A Note on the Making

Every commonplace book should say, at the back, how it was bound. Here is how this one was.

This is a commonplace book kept in public: part anthology of invented stories, part working notebook, part drawer of borrowed sentences. The index page is meant to feel like a writer's desk — layered, a little dense, rewarding a second look. Every entry, by contrast, is a quiet room with a single lamp: one column, comfortable measure, nothing in the way of the words.

How it's built

Plain HTML, one stylesheet, and a little vanilla JavaScript — no framework, no build step, no dependencies to rot. Each entry is a real HTML file with its own address, so you can link straight to a story and it will always be there. The homepage reads a small catalog file and renders the feed, the search, and the tag filter from it.

Because there's no build step, you can open index.html straight off your hard drive and it works. Nothing to compile, nothing to serve.

The type

Titles are set in Fraunces, a characterful literary serif with a slight wonk to its letterforms — the handwritten feel of a good headline. The body is Inter, a clean humanist sans chosen so that reading a long story never tires the eye. Dates, tags, and marginal notes are set in IBM Plex Mono, the typewriter voice of the margins.

The palette

Four colours, chosen to feel like ink and aged paper rather than a screen:

  • Bone #EDE7DA — the paper.
  • Ink #211D26 — the words.
  • Oxblood #7A2E2E — titles and links.
  • Verdigris #4C7A6F — tags and hover.

A single warm ochre joins them for tiny ornaments — the asterisks between sections, a star on a map. Everything else is a tint or shade of the original four, so the page has depth without ever going off-key.

On comfort & motion

The site is responsive to the last hinge of a phone, and it listens for prefers-reduced-motion: if your system asks for stillness, the gentle reveals and hovers simply don't happen, and nothing is lost. Contrast, focus outlines, and a skip link are all in place, because a commonplace book is no use if you can't read it.

Adding your own entries

The whole thing is yours to keep writing in. To add a piece: copy any file in the entries folder, write your story into it, and register it at the top of js/entries.js — one small block with a title, date, tags, and a line of searchable text. It will appear on the index, join the search, and slot into the newer/older navigation automatically. Full instructions live in the README.
